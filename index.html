Who I am

I'm a first-year at MIT studying Computer Science and Political Science. Egyptian-American, first-gen, grew up in Northern California. Diagnosed with congenital nystagmus (involuntary eye movement) in high school, so I learned to code to fix my own problem.

That experience taught me to move fast, solve real problems, and build with urgency. I believe the best products come from lived experience. I move fast—usually fueled by coffee and loud music, often building late into the night.

Now focused on Plateform (universal nutrition layer), vision research (understanding oscillation), and thinking about the next 10x problem. Long-term: build wealth to control time and make lasting impact.
