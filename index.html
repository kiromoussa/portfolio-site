About

I'm a first-year at MIT studying Computer Science and Political Science. Egyptian-American, first-generation, grew up in Northern California. I built my first company in high school after being diagnosed with congenital nystagmus — a vision disorder that caused my eyes to move involuntarily.

That experience taught me how to solve real problems fast. I learned to code to fix my own problem. I learned to lead by necessity. I learned that the best products come from lived experience, not market research.

Now I'm focused on Plateform (building the universal nutrition layer for restaurants), vision research (understanding how oscillation helps people see clearer), and thinking about the next 10x problem. I move fast, learn by doing, and believe the best founders are builders first, strategists second.
