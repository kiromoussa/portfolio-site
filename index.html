02 — About

Who I am.

b. Egypt · raised CA

I'm Kiro — Egyptian-American, MIT Class of 2029, studying Computer Science, Data Science & Economics. I came to MIT as a QuestBridge Scholar from Hemet, California.

I'm building Plateform because finding a meal at MIT dining shouldn't be harder than streaming a movie. I'm also finishing two years of research using computer vision to help people with nystagmus — a condition my sister and I both have — read without strain. It works for 12+ people.

Before all of this: I ran a Minecraft server on a salvaged laptop at age twelve. That's when I understood I didn't just want to use software—I wanted to control it.

I lived in Egypt for the first seven years of my life. I'm fluent in Arabic and English. I move fast—usually fueled by coffee and loud music, often building late into the night. Long-term: build wealth to control time and make lasting impact.
